Overview

Leading UX across 200+ schools.

Spring Education Group operates over 200 private school websites — the primary way prospective families find and inquire about enrollment. I led the UX redesign of multiple school websites focused on improving Visit-to-Inquiry (V2I) conversion and the overall parent experience across desktop and mobile.

Using GA4 analytics, heuristic evaluation, competitive analysis, parent interviews, and surveys, we identified key usability barriers and redesigned critical user flows.

About SEG

A network of schools built for families.

Spring Education Group operates private schools across the United States, serving families from early childhood through high school. Each school has its own grade levels, geographic market, and brand — but they all share the same core need: a website that converts prospective parents into inquiries.

These websites had to do three things well:

  • Encourage inquiry submissions
  • Communicate each school's value proposition clearly
  • Build trust with prospective families fast

Research & Discovery

Listening before designing.

We ran surveys and interviews with prospective parents to understand what they were actually looking for when researching schools, what drove their decisions, and what was getting in their way.

Key insights from parents
  • Clear value communication immediately upon landing
  • Faster access to critical information
  • Stronger emotional connection to the school

Competitive Analysis

Understanding the landscape.

I reviewed competitor school websites to see what they were doing well and where SEG had room to stand out. The patterns were consistent across every strong performer:

->Clear CTAs above the fold
->Strong visual storytelling with authentic photography
->Clearly communicated unique value propositions
Design Process

From research to wireframes to hi-fi.

Early on I sketched out wireframes exploring new layout directions that put value messaging front and center and made the inquiry path obvious. I also built out distinct color systems tied to each school's brand identity while keeping accessibility in check.

We went through several rounds of iteration, bringing designs back to stakeholders regularly. Some stakeholders pushed for a uniform look across all schools — we used the research to make the case for why stronger differentiation actually helped parents connect with the right school faster.

Building within WordPress meant the dev team had to be part of the conversation from day one. I kept everything annotated in Figma Dev Mode and ran weekly syncs with engineering to catch technical constraints before they turned into redesign requests.

Final Design & Handoff

Handed off using Figma Dev Mode.

Reflection

A career-defining project.

This project was a turning point. The scope, the depth of research, and the results it produced all played a role in my promotion to Senior UX Designer.

More than anything else I had worked on, this pushed me to think and operate at a strategic level. Grounding decisions in data, working through competing priorities with stakeholders, and staying with a project from early discovery all the way through post-launch measurement.

Designing across 200+ school brands while keeping everything feeling cohesive was a real balancing act. WordPress shaped design decisions more than I would have liked — certain patterns just weren't feasible in the CMS, which meant finding workarounds and having honest conversations about what would make it into v1.

  • Leading UX strategy end-to-end across a large organization
  • Aligning design decisions directly to business goals
  • Translating research insights into measurable outcomes
  • Collaborating cross-functionally across design, product, engineering, and marketing
